Bathukamma


Bathukamma is a vibrant festival that celebrates the relationship between humans, water, and the earth. It is celebrated by women in Telangana, India, and is a symbol of the state's cultural identity. The festival takes place during the latter half of monsoon, usually in September or October.

Here are some things to know about Bathukamma:

Celebrations:

Women gather in large groups to celebrate with their Bathukammas. They sing folk songs around the Bathukamma, and offer special dishes called naivedyam to the goddess each day.

Attire:

Women dress in traditional saris with jewels and accessories, while teenage girls wear half-sarees, lehenga cholis, or langa-vonis with jewels.

Floral arrangements:

Flowers are arranged on a plate called a Vistharaku. If the flowers don't have strong stems, pieces of broomsticks are used to hold them together. A pumpkin flower is placed at the top.

Farewell:

At the end of the festival, Bathukamma is given a traditional farewell called Saaganampadam. It is placed over a body of water, such as a river or lake, and the plate is removed. The Bathukamma floats on the water.
